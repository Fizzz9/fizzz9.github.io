About Me

I work on computer vision and multimodal modeling, focusing on generative vision models and their integration with LLMs/VLMs. I leverage geometry and video priors to ground models, and use language and visual prompts for controllable perception and generation.

My long-term goal is to build coherent vision–language systems for embodied AI: grounding with geometric and temporal consistency, aligning perception with instruction following, and providing interpretable control, so that agents can perceive–plan–act with reliable transfer across tasks and environments.

Publications

HumanGenesis: Agent-Based Geometric and Generative Modeling for Synthetic Human Dynamics
Weiqi Li, Zehao Zhang, Liang Lin, Guangrun Wang

HumanGenesis develops a high-fidelity human synthesis pipeline that couples geometric reconstruction with generative refinement, enabling temporally consistent dynamic sequences from monocular inputs. A Reflector component introduces human-in-the-loop feedback to correct reconstruction drift and enhance perceptual fidelity, yielding stable, detailed renderings for animation and reenactment.
